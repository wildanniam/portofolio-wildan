# Portfolio V4 Design Lock

- Status: direction locked; high-fidelity visual frames still require Human Visual Gate 1 approval
- Register: high-expression brand UI
- Working concept: **Research / Build Atlas**
- Language: English

## Product promise

The portfolio must make Wildan memorable as an AI researcher who turns technical questions into working software, especially across AI agents, software engineering, and blockchain infrastructure.

It is not a CV, a generic developer landing page, a trophy wall, a terminal simulation, or a fictional product company.

## Audience

Primary:

- AI researchers and research collaborators;
- AI/Web3 founders and technical product teams;
- software engineering recruiters evaluating system thinking and execution;
- technical communities and builder programs.

The site must convince a skeptical technical visitor without sounding defensive or inflated.

## Five-second, thirty-second, and two-minute outcomes

Within five seconds a visitor must understand:

1. this is Wildan Syukri Niam's portfolio;
2. Wildan is an **AI Researcher & Web3 Builder**;
3. the primary action is exploring his work.

Within thirty seconds:

1. Fradium, Nova, PayGate, and Quorum are visible as one collection;
2. each project has a distinct identity, question, flow, role, and outcome;
3. PayGate is recognizable as the project currently being shipped.

Within two minutes:

1. a visitor can enter a case study and understand the problem, build, role, system, and result;
2. a visitor can return to the Atlas or continue to the next project;
3. Moments, research direction, GitHub, and contact are easy to find.

### Project-stage contract

Every stage contains, in the same semantic boundary:

1. authentic logo or mark;
2. project name;
3. one research or product question;
4. a one-sentence product answer;
5. Wildan's approved role;
6. one strongest outcome;
7. one bounded visual workflow made from authentic product fragments;
8. an explicit case-study action.

Overlap is allowed only inside the project's visual stage. Every stage uses `overflow: clip`. A project's layers may never enter another project's copy or visual boundary.

Meaningful UI screenshots use intrinsic or contained presentation. They are never blindly rendered as `object-fit: cover`.

## Visual language

The portfolio remains cohesive through a paper/ink interface, expressive typography, documentary photography, and precise structure. Project brand colors live primarily inside their own visual stages.

The redesign must add depth through composition, imagery, texture, connector systems, and controlled layering—not generic gradients, glass cards, floating orbs, or decorative 3D.

Target dials:

- visual variance: 8 / 10;
- motion: 7 / 10;
- content density: 5 / 10;
- surface decoration: 3 / 10.

### Typography gate

V3's typography is not automatically retained. Before implementation, two expressive but licensable pairings must be rendered in the approved hero and Atlas frame:

1. strong variable grotesk + restrained editorial italic;
2. characterful grotesk-only system with width/weight contrast.

The winning pair must pass desktop and 390px review before tokens are frozen. A serif may accent one line; it may not make the entire site feel like a fashion magazine.

### Color

- Portfolio canvas: warm paper or a nearby neutral approved in the visual frame.
- Primary text: deep graphite, not pure black.
- Interface accent: restrained cobalt used for navigation, focus, and authored annotations.
- Project stage accents:
  - Fradium: green risk chamber;
  - Nova: violet intent console;
  - PayGate: purple/cyan payment rail;
  - Quorum: black/cyan settlement field.

Project colors may not leak into global navigation or compete as four unrelated site themes.

## Authentic asset rules

- Logos identify; product fragments prove.
- Maximum per homepage stage: mark, optional wordmark, one primary surface, two supporting fragments, one connector.
- No full-page screenshot is used as a generic cover.
- No raster logo is enlarged beyond a defensible source resolution.
- Nova's 64px mark cannot be used as an oversized centerpiece. A faithful vector trace or high-resolution source must pass visual overlay comparison first.
- Quorum's white logo is used on its intended dark stage.
- Original shape and proportions of all project logos remain intact.
- Source repository, revision, path, creator, and rights are recorded for every imported asset.

## Signature motion: Signal → System

Static HTML always contains the complete final composition. Motion progressively explains causality:

1. project mark resolves;
2. a connector forms;
3. product fragments assemble in workflow order;
4. outcome and action settle.

Project-specific choreography:

- Fradium: signals converge into a risk verdict;
- Nova: prompt becomes a proposed action and stops at user approval;
- PayGate: request packet moves through 402, payment, and 200 response;
- Quorum: event, pass, split, and proof settle into one coordination flow.

Rules:

- no pinning;
- no smooth-scroll hijacking;
- no long scrub sequence;
- no horizontal fake scroll;
- no custom cursor;
- no infinite decorative loop;
- stage entrance plays once and completes within 900ms;
- pointer depth is limited to 4–6px on fine pointers;
- focus and touch receive complete, non-hover-dependent content;
- reduced motion shows the final state immediately.

## Responsive contract

### Desktop: 1280 and 1440

- 12-column authored composition.
- Atlas remains asymmetric and bounded.
- Media and copy have explicit ownership boundaries.

### Tablet: 768 and 1024

- 8-column layout with deliberate recomposition.
- 768 may stack stages when two columns harm legibility.
- No desktop absolute-position logic survives if it creates overlap.

### Mobile: 320, 360, 390, and 430

- Hero order: identity → headline → support → actions → portrait.
- Project stage order: copy → visual flow → outcome/action.
- One primary artifact per project; supporting details move to the case study.
- No pointer parallax.
- No reordered media that changes project association.
- No horizontal overflow at 320px or 200% zoom.

## Accessibility

- WCAG 2.2 AA contrast.
- One `h1` and coherent heading order.
- Visible focus on every project background.
- All actions work with keyboard and touch.
- Logo beside a textual project name is decorative to assistive technology.
- Meaningful scenes receive one concise accessible summary; decorative fragments are hidden.
- Motion does not carry exclusive information.
- Dialogs retain title, description, Escape, focus trap, and focus return.
- Reduced-motion behavior is complete, not a degraded empty state.

## Explicit exclusions

- No screenshot-cover Atlas.
- No V3 blue proof ribbon.
- No generic bento or equal card collection.
- No glassmorphism, blobs, floating orbs, or fake dashboards.
- No Three.js, R3F, WebGL, or canvas requirement.
- No viewport-blocking project preview.
- No defensive disclaimer wall on the landing page.
- No autonomous full implementation before the approved golden slice.
